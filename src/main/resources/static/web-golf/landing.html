Wooden golf club

Traditional wood clubs are typically used to hit the ball long distances off the tee. They are the largest clubs in the golf bag and have a rounded head that resembles the shape of a barrel. They are available in different numberings.

Iron golf clubs

Iron clubs are used for a variety of shots, such as from the fairway, for approaches to the green, bunker shots and for longer putts. They have smaller, narrower heads than wood clubs and their numbering is assigned according to the angle of the clubface.

Hybrid golf clubs

Hybrid clubs resemble a combination of wood and iron clubs. They have a rounded head like a wood club and a shorter shaft length, but also have a tighter clubface angle like iron clubs.They are designed to help golfers get more distance and accuracy.

Learn a little about the field

Tee Box

This is the area where the golfer places the ball at the beginning of the hole, and from where he makes his first shot.

Fairway

The widest and cleanest area of the hole extending from the tee to the green. This area is covered by short, uniform grass and is where the ball is intended to arrive with as few strokes as possible.

Rough

The area around the fairway with longer and rougher grass. The ball can sink into the rough, making it more difficult to hit and control.

Bunker (Sand Trap)

An area of sand with an irregular surface surrounding the green. It is designed to be an obstacle and a challenge for golfers.

Green

It is the area where the flag is located, and where the golfer must try to get the ball in as few strokes as possible. It is a flat and well-kept surface, usually circular or rectangular in shape.

Approach (Approach Zone)

This is the area between the green and the fairway. It is an area in which the golfer must make a precise shot in order to leave the ball in a good position to make a putt.

Hazards

These can be water, streams, lakes, rivers, etc., which are located in different areas of the course and are considered obstacles for the player. If the ball lands on any of these hazards, a penalty is lost.

Putting Green

It is the flat and well-kept area surrounding the flag on the green. It is where putts are made and where the hole ends.
